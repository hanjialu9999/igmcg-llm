What is Nash equilibrium?
A stable outcome in a strategic game where no player can benefit by changing only their own strategy.
What is the prisoners' dilemma and why does it matter in economics?
Two parties each choose an individually rational action that leads to a collectively worse outcome.
How do expectations of future earnings affect current asset prices?
Investors discount future cash flows to estimate what an asset is worth today.
What is the economic impact of a minimum wage increase?
It raises income for low-wage workers but may reduce employment if set too high.
How do network effects create winner-take-all markets?
The more people use a platform, the more valuable it becomes, attracting even more users.
What is the concept of market failure?
When a free market fails to allocate resources efficiently on its own.
How does a recession affect government finances?
Tax revenues fall and spending on social programs rises, widening the fiscal deficit.
What is the difference between a liquid and illiquid asset?
Liquid assets can be quickly converted to cash while illiquid ones take time to sell.
How does the financial system allocate resources in an economy?
It channels savings toward their most productive uses through lending and investment.
What is the role of price signals in a market economy?
Prices convey information about scarcity and value, guiding producers and consumers.
How does a lack of competition harm innovation?
Without competitive pressure, firms have less incentive to invest in new products or processes.
What is an economic sanction?
A trade penalty imposed by one country or group on another to change its behavior.
How does inequality of opportunity differ from inequality of outcome?
Opportunity inequality reflects unequal starting points while outcome inequality reflects unequal results.
What is the role of the bond market in funding government activity?
Governments sell bonds to investors to finance spending beyond what taxes collect.
How does consumer protection law affect market efficiency?
It corrects information imbalances and prevents exploitation, increasing trust in markets.
What is the accrual basis of accounting?
Revenue and expenses are recorded when they occur, not when cash changes hands.
How does debt restructuring help a struggling country?
It renegotiates repayment terms to reduce the burden and allow the economy to stabilize.
What is the Mundell-Fleming model?
It describes how monetary and fiscal policies interact with exchange rates in an open economy.
How does globalization affect income inequality within countries?
It can raise average incomes but often increases wage gaps between skilled and unskilled workers.
What is the economic concept of creative finance?
Using innovative financial structures to fund projects in unconventional ways.
How does a lack of savings affect long-term investment?
Without domestic savings, countries must rely on foreign capital, which can be unreliable.
What is the economic significance of political institutions?
They determine the rules of the economic game, affecting investment, trade, and distribution.
How does the principle of revealed preference work?
Economists infer what people value from their actual choices rather than their stated preferences.
