What is Chinese language?
Chinese language is a Sino-Tibetan language spoken by over a billion people primarily in China.
What is Mandarin Chinese?
Mandarin Chinese is the most widely spoken variety of Chinese and the official language of China.
What is a Chinese character?
A Chinese character is a logographic writing unit representing a morpheme or word.

There are over fifty thousand Chinese characters but only a few thousand are commonly used.

Pinyin is the romanization system for transcribing Mandarin Chinese pronunciation.

What is a radical in Chinese?
A radical is a component of a Chinese character used for dictionary classification.

The six categories are pictographs, ideographs, compound ideographs, phonetic compounds, derivative cognates, and phonetic loans.
What is a pictograph?
A pictograph is a character that visually resembles the object it represents.
What is an ideograph?
An ideograph is a character representing an abstract concept or idea.
What is a phonetic compound?
A phonetic compound combines a semantic radical with a phonetic component.
What is traditional Chinese?
Traditional Chinese uses the original complex character forms preserved in Taiwan and Hong Kong.
What is simplified Chinese?
Simplified Chinese uses reduced character forms adopted in mainland China since the nineteen fifties.
What is Classical Chinese?
Classical Chinese is the written language used in ancient China until the early twentieth century.
What is vernacular Chinese?
Vernacular Chinese is written language based on spoken forms developed in the early twentieth century.
What is a measure word?
A measure word is a classifier used between numbers and nouns in Chinese.
What is Chinese grammar?
Chinese grammar follows subject-verb-object order with no verb conjugation or noun inflection.
What is aspect in Chinese?
Aspect markers indicate whether an action is completed, ongoing, or experiential.

Semi-cursive script is a calligraphic style between regular and cursive scripts.
