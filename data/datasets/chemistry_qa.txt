What is retrosynthetic analysis?
Retrosynthetic analysis is working backward from target molecule to available starting materials.
What is a synthon?
A synthon is an idealized fragment used in retrosynthetic analysis representing a synthetic equivalent.
What is a protecting group?
A protecting group temporarily masks a reactive functional group during multi-step synthesis.
What is regioselectivity?
Regioselectivity is the preferential formation of one constitutional isomer over others in a reaction.
What is stereoselectivity?
Stereoselectivity is the preferential formation of one stereoisomer over others in a reaction.
What is enantioselectivity?
Enantioselectivity is the preferential formation of one enantiomer over the other in a reaction.
What is asymmetric synthesis?
Asymmetric synthesis produces chiral molecules with preferential formation of one enantiomer.
What is a chiral auxiliary?
A chiral auxiliary is a chiral group temporarily attached to guide stereoselective reactions.
What is enzymatic resolution?
Enzymatic resolution separates enantiomers using enzyme specificity for one enantiomer.
What is kinetic resolution?
Kinetic resolution separates enantiomers by selective reaction of one enantiomer at a faster rate.
What is dynamic kinetic resolution?
Dynamic kinetic resolution combines racemization with kinetic resolution to convert all starting material to one product.
What is organometallic chemistry?
Organometallic chemistry studies compounds containing metal-carbon bonds.
What is a Grignard reagent?
A Grignard reagent is an organomagnesium compound used as a strong nucleophile in organic synthesis.
What is a Gilman reagent?
A Gilman reagent is an organocuprate compound used for conjugate addition and coupling reactions.
What is the Suzuki coupling?
The Suzuki coupling is a palladium-catalyzed cross-coupling between organoboron compounds and organic halides.
What is the Heck reaction?
The Heck reaction is a palladium-catalyzed coupling between alkenes and aryl or vinyl halides.
What is metathesis?
Metathesis is a catalytic reaction where substituents are exchanged between similar chemical species.
What is olefin metathesis?
Olefin metathesis exchanges alkylidene groups between alkenes using metal carbene catalysts.
What is ring-opening metathesis polymerization?
ROMP is polymerization of cyclic olefins through successive metathesis reactions creating polymer chains.
What is the Grubbs catalyst?
The Grubbs catalyst is a ruthenium-based carbene complex used for olefin metathesis reactions.
What is oxidative addition?
Oxidative addition is insertion of a substrate into a metal complex increasing oxidation state by two.
What is reductive elimination?
Reductive elimination is ejection of two ligands from a metal forming a new bond and reducing metal oxidation state.
What is transmetallation?
Transmetallation is transfer of organic groups from one metal to another.
What is beta-hydride elimination?
Beta-hydride elimination is removal of a beta hydrogen forming a metal hydride and an alkene.
What is migratory insertion?
Migratory insertion is a reaction where a ligand inserts into a metal-ligand bond.
What is the eighteen-electron rule?
The eighteen-electron rule states that stable transition metal complexes tend to have eighteen valence electrons.
What is a pi-complex?
A pi-complex is a coordination compound where a pi system coordinates to a metal center.
What is back-bonding?
Back-bonding is electron donation from filled metal d-orbitals to empty pi-antibonding orbitals of ligands.
What is synergistic bonding?
Synergistic bonding involves both sigma donation from ligand to metal and pi back-donation from metal to ligand.
What is medicinal chemistry?
Medicinal chemistry is the design and development of pharmaceutical drugs.
What is a pharmacophore?
A pharmacophore is the ensemble of molecular features necessary for biological activity.
What is drug metabolism?
Drug metabolism is biochemical modification of drugs by living organisms typically making them more water-soluble for excretion.
What are cytochrome P450 enzymes?
Cytochrome P450 enzymes are a family of enzymes responsible for metabolizing most drugs in the body.
What is bioavailability?
Bioavailability is the fraction of administered drug that reaches systemic circulation unchanged.
What is the blood-brain barrier?
The blood-brain barrier is a selective membrane preventing most substances from entering the brain from blood.
What is a prodrug?
A prodrug is an inactive compound that becomes active after metabolic conversion in the body.
What is structure-activity relationship?
SAR correlates changes in molecular structure with changes in biological activity.
What is Lipinski's rule of five?
Lipinski's rule predicts drug-likeness based on molecular weight, lipophilicity, hydrogen bond donors, and acceptors.
What is high-throughput screening?
High-throughput screening rapidly tests large numbers of compounds for biological activity.
What is combinatorial chemistry?
Combinatorial chemistry synthesizes large libraries of related compounds systematically.
What is fragment-based drug discovery?
Fragment-based drug discovery identifies small molecular fragments that bind weakly then elaborates them into drug candidates.
